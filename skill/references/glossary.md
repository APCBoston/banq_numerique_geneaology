# Glossary — standard French→English equivalents

Reuse these consistently across a whole register so the translation reads as one hand. This same list (trimmed to the terms that actually appear) makes a good `## Glossary` section at the foot of the output document.

## Dates and birth/death timing

- **né / née la veille** — born the day before
- **né(e) de ce jour / le même jour / d'aujourd'hui** — born this same day
- **né(e) l'avant-veille** — born two days before
- **du courant / du mois** — of the current month
- **du mois dernier / dernier** — of last (the previous) month
- **décédé(e) la veille / l'avant-veille** — died the day before / two days before
- **âgé(e) de … ans / mois / jours** — aged … years / months / days

## Baptism terms

- **avons baptisé** — [we] baptized
- **né(e) et ondoyé(e)** — born and given a private (emergency) lay baptism
- **ondoyé(e) à la maison** — privately (lay) baptized at home (e.g., by a relative or the doctor, in danger of death)
- **ondoyé(e) par le docteur** — given emergency lay baptism by the doctor
- **baptisé(e) sous condition** — baptized conditionally (when an earlier emergency baptism is in doubt)
- **avons suppléé les cérémonies du baptême** — [we] supplied the church ceremonies of baptism afterward (for a child already ondoyé)
- **du légitime mariage de … et de …** — of the lawful marriage of … and …
- **de parents inconnus** — of unknown parents (a foundling)
- **parrain / marraine** — godfather / godmother

## Marriage terms

- **publication de(s) bans** — publication of the banns of marriage
- **après la publication de trois bans / d'un ban** — after the publication of three banns / one banns
- **dispense de deux bans** — dispensation from two of the banns
- **dispense de consanguinité du troisième au troisième degré** — dispensation for consanguinity in the third-to-third degree (spouses are third cousins)
- **comme il appert par le certificat de … curé du lieu** — as appears from the certificate of … the local parish priest (proof banns were also called in the other party's parish)
- **vicaire général** — vicar general (a bishop's chief deputy; could grant dispensations)
- **avons reçu leur mutuel consentement de mariage** — [we] received their mutual consent of marriage
- **avons donné la bénédiction nuptiale** — [we] gave them the nuptial blessing
- **du consentement des parties intéressées** — with the consent of the interested parties
- **ne s'étant découvert aucun empêchement** — no impediment having been discovered
- **fils / fille majeur(e)** — son / daughter of legal age
- **fils / fille mineur(e)** — son / daughter under legal age
- **domicilié(e) en cette paroisse / à …** — residing in this parish / at …

## Burial terms

- **avons inhumé dans le cimetière de cette paroisse le corps de …** — [we] buried in the cemetery of this parish the body of …
- **avons suppléé les cérémonies de la sépulture** — [we] supplied the (church) burial ceremonies afterward (for someone already buried without them, e.g., in winter)
- **furent présents …** — present were …
- **époux / épouse de** — husband / wife of
- **époux / épouse de feu(e)** — husband / wife of the late …

## People, status, occupations

- **feu / feue / défunt(e)** — the late (deceased)
- **dit / dite** — "called/alias," a secondary family surname (e.g., Therrien dit Lépine)
- **beau-frère / belle-sœur** — brother-in-law / sister-in-law
- **oncle / tante** — uncle / aunt; **ami(e)** — friend; **voisin(e)** — neighbour
- **cultivateur** — farmer; **fermier** — farmer (tenant); **journalier** — day-labourer
- **menuisier** — carpenter; **charpentier** — house-carpenter; **forgeron** — blacksmith
- **maçon** — mason; **sellier** — saddler; **tanneur** — tanner; **meunier** — miller
- **marchand** — merchant; **voiturier** — carter / teamster; **architecte** — builder/architect
- **instituteur** — schoolteacher; **bedeau** — beadle / church sexton
- **député registrateur** — deputy registrar (of deeds); **juge de paix** — justice of the peace
- **médecin / docteur** — physician/doctor

## Signatures and clerk's notes

- **soussigné** — undersigned
- **qui n'ont pas signé** — who did not sign (most rural parishioners were illiterate)
- **qui n'ont pu signer** — who could not sign (equivalent; the later vicar uses this form)
- **a déclaré ne savoir signer** — declared he/she could not sign
- **ont signé avec nous** — signed with us
- **un / deux mot(s) rayé(s) nul(s)** — one / two crossed-out words [declared] void
- **un mot interligné bon** — one interlined (inserted-between-lines) word valid
- **un renvoi en marge bon** — one marginal insertion [declared] valid

## Clergy and officials

- **ptre (prêtre)** — priest
- **ptre vic. (prêtre vicaire)** — priest-vicar
- **curé** — parish priest
- **Messire / Mgr (Monseigneur)** — honorifics (for a priest / a bishop)
- **P.C.** — Prothonotary of the Court; **P.C.S.** — Prothonotary of the Superior Court
