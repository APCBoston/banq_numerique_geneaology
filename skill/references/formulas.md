# Standard entry formulas (French templates + English)

These mid-19th-century Quebec acts are highly formulaic. Knowing the skeleton lets you focus reading effort on the variable parts — the **names, dates, places, occupations, and relationships** — which is where errors and uncertainty actually live. The boilerplate below is what to expect; fill the brackets from the image and keep the clerk's exact wording where it varies.

Names in `‹angle brackets›` are the variables you read off the page. Don't copy the angle brackets into the output.

---

## Baptism (B)

**French skeleton:**
> Le ‹date en lettres› mil huit cent ‹année›, nous prêtre soussigné avons baptisé ‹prénoms›, né(e) ‹la veille / de ce jour / l'avant-veille›, du légitime mariage de ‹père›, ‹métier›, et de ‹mère›, de ‹lieu›. Le parrain a été ‹parrain› et la marraine ‹marraine›, qui[, ainsi que le père,] n'ont pas signé[. Le père absent]. ‹signature du prêtre›

**English skeleton:**
> On the ‹date› 18‹yy›, we the undersigned priest baptized ‹given names›, born ‹the day before / this day / two days earlier›, of the lawful marriage of ‹father›, ‹occupation›, and ‹mother›, of ‹place›. The godfather was ‹godfather› and the godmother ‹godmother›, who[, like the father,] did not sign[. The father absent]. ‹priest signature›

Variants: *avons suppléé les cérémonies du baptême à ‹nom›, ondoyé(e) à la maison par ‹nom›* (church ceremonies supplied for a child already privately baptized); *baptisé sous condition* (baptized conditionally); *de parents inconnus* (foundling — no parents named).

## Marriage (M)

**French skeleton:**
> Le ‹date› mil huit cent ‹année›, après la publication de ‹trois bans / un ban› de mariage faite au prône de nos messes paroissiales[, la dispense des ‹deux autres bans› ayant été accordée par ‹autorité› en vertu des pouvoirs … par Sa Grandeur Monseigneur Thomas Cooke, évêque des Trois-Rivières], entre ‹marié›, ‹métier/domicile›, fils ‹majeur/mineur› de ‹père du marié›, ‹métier›, et de ‹mère du marié›, de ‹lieu›, d'une part; et ‹mariée›, fille ‹majeure/mineure› de ‹père de la mariée›, ‹métier›, et de ‹mère de la mariée›, de ‹lieu›, d'autre part; ne s'étant découvert aucun empêchement au dit mariage, nous prêtre ‹curé/vicaire› soussigné avons[, du consentement des parties intéressées,] reçu leur mutuel consentement de mariage et leur avons donné la bénédiction nuptiale, en présence de ‹témoin 1, lien› et de ‹témoin 2, lien›, qui, ainsi que les deux contractants, ‹n'ont pas signé / ont signé avec nous›. ‹signatures› ‹signature du prêtre›

**English skeleton:**
> On the ‹date› 18‹yy›, after the publication of ‹three banns / one banns› of marriage made at the sermon of our parish masses[, dispensation from the ‹other two banns› having been granted by ‹authority› by virtue of the powers … by His Grace Bishop Thomas Cooke, Bishop of Trois-Rivières], between ‹groom›, ‹occupation/residence›, ‹of-age/minor› son of ‹groom's father›, ‹occupation›, and ‹groom's mother›, of ‹place›, on the one part; and ‹bride›, ‹of-age/minor› daughter of ‹bride's father›, ‹occupation›, and ‹bride's mother›, of ‹place›, on the other part; no impediment to the said marriage having been discovered, we the undersigned ‹parish priest / priest-vicar›[, with the consent of the interested parties,] received their mutual consent of marriage and gave them the nuptial blessing, in the presence of ‹witness 1, relationship› and ‹witness 2, relationship›, who, like the two contracting parties, ‹did not sign / signed with us›. ‹signatures› ‹priest signature›

Note: when banns were also called in another parish, the act cites *le certificat du Révérend Messire ‹nom›, curé du lieu* — capture that priest and parish too, as it points to where the other spouse came from.

## Burial / Sépulture (S)

**French skeleton:**
> Le ‹date› mil huit cent ‹année›, nous prêtre soussigné avons inhumé dans le cimetière de ‹cette paroisse / St-Camille› le corps de ‹nom›, ‹époux/épouse de … OR enfant de ‹père›, ‹métier›, et de ‹mère››, de ‹lieu›, décédé(e) ‹la veille / l'avant-veille / le ‹date››, à l'âge de ‹âge›. Furent présents ‹témoin 1› et ‹témoin 2›, qui n'ont pas signé. ‹signature du prêtre›

**English skeleton:**
> On the ‹date› 18‹yy›, we the undersigned priest buried in the cemetery of ‹this parish / St-Camille› the body of ‹name›, ‹husband/wife of … OR child of ‹father›, ‹occupation›, and ‹mother››, of ‹place›, who died ‹the day before / two days earlier / on ‹date››, aged ‹age›. Present were ‹witness 1› and ‹witness 2›, who did not sign. ‹priest signature›

Variant: *avons suppléé les cérémonies de la sépulture sur le corps de ‹nom›, décédé(e) le ‹date› et inhumé(e) le ‹date›* — church burial ceremonies supplied later for someone already interred (common after winter burials).

The two recurring witnesses in many parishes are the sexton/beadle and a neighbour (e.g., "Antoine Jourdain et Rodolphe Ducharme"); reading one confidently often helps you read the other.

## Court certification (usually the first leaf)

Frequently pre-printed/handwritten in **English** in Quebec. Transcribe verbatim, expand abbreviations, and record any law stamp and seal. Typical wording:

> Province of Canada / District of ‹district› — This Register containing ‹n› leaves is to be used for inregistering the Baptisms, Marriages and Burials in the Roman Catholic ‹Mission/Parish› of ‹name› … for the year ‹year in words›. Witness the seal of the Superior Court and our signature set at ‹place›, this ‹date›. ‹Prothonotary›, P.C.

If French, it follows the *Nous, Protonotaire de la Cour Supérieure … certifions que ce Registre …* pattern; translate it like any other entry.
